Education in India is primarily managed by state-run public education system, which fall under the command of the government at three levels: central, state and local. With the advent of Islam in India the traditional methods of education increasingly came under Islamic influence.[32] Pre-Mughal rulers such as Qutb-ud-din Aybak and other Muslim rulers initiated institutions which imparted religious knowledge.[32] Scholars such as Nizamuddin Auliya and Moinuddin Chishti became prominent educators and established Islamic monasteries.[32] Students from Bukhara and Afghanistan visited India to study humanities and science.[32] Islamic institution of education in India included traditional madrassas and maktabs which taught grammar, philosophy, mathematics, and law influenced by the Greek traditions inherited by Persia.
